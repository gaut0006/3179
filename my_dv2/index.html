Alcohol Use, Risk & Outcomes by State (AU)

Alcohol plays a complex role in Australian life,woven into celebrations, culture, and community, yet responsible for substantial health and social harm. This project visualises patterns of alcohol consumption, risk, and outcomes across Australian states from national datasets. By combining interactive maps and charts, it highlights how drinking behaviour and alcohol-induced deaths vary geographically and over time, revealing where the burden of alcohol harm falls most heavily.

Mapping the Burden of Alcohol-Related Mortality Across Australia

Mapping the Burden of Alcohol-Related Mortality Across Australia

The map highlights striking geographic disparities in alcohol-related mortality across Australia. The Northern Territory consistently records the highest death rates, reflecting the combined impact of social disadvantage, accessibility of alcohol, and limited health service availability in remote areas. In contrast, larger states like New South Wales and Victoria show lower rates, suggesting policy and urban health infrastructure may mitigate some risks.
